The vestibular hair cell simulation project

An attempt to understand the functioning of the vestibular systems sensory cells.

Welcome to The vestibular hair cell simulation project. The goal of this project is building software models to help understand the functioning of vestibular systems hair cells. On this website, you will find information about the project, code and data I use for the project. You will also find reviews on scientific papers related to the project.

About the project

Why did you come up with a science project about sensory cells in the vestibular system?

Sensory nerve cells, in general, serve a clear role in transforming external stimulus into a signal which is propagated into the brain. Sensory nerve cells transform external stimulus into an altered sub-currents in the cell. The membrane potential of the cell changes as a result of the sub-currents alteration. The rate of release of neurotransmitter is altered and that is how the signal is passed into the brain.

Why study the vestibular nerve cells in particular?

Vestibular nerve cells are interesting because they transform the force of gravity into signals which help us to know which way is up and way is down. in general, the vestibular system helps us orientate in 3D space.
